# Quirkable Business Model Canvas

**Strategic Business Planning Framework**

---

## Business Model Canvas Overview

The Business Model Canvas is a strategic management template for developing new business models and documenting existing ones. It provides a visual chart with elements describing Quirkable's value proposition, infrastructure, customers, and finances.

---

## 1. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS

**Who are we creating value for? Who are our most important customers?**

### Primary Segments

**Hobbyists & Makers (30% of market)**
- Demographics: Ages 18-55, tech-savvy, creative
- Needs: High-quality prints for gaming, cosplay, collections
- Challenges: File errors, printer limitations, design skills
- Value: Affordable quality, file repair, exclusive models
- Size: Large and growing

**Small Businesses & Startups (25% of market)**
- Demographics: 1-50 employees, product development stage
- Needs: Rapid prototyping, iteration, professional quality
- Challenges: Cost, speed, design expertise
- Value: Fast turnaround, design support, confidentiality
- Size: High value per customer

**Gift Buyers (20% of market)**
- Demographics: Ages 25-60, seeking unique presents
- Needs: Personalized, unique, thoughtful gifts
- Challenges: Finding truly unique items
- Value: Customization, ready-made options, quality
- Size: Seasonal peaks, recurring occasions

**DIY Innovators (15% of market)**
- Demographics: Ages 30-65, practical, sustainability-focused
- Needs: Replacement parts, repairs, custom solutions
- Challenges: Discontinued parts, finding alternatives
- Value: Custom manufacturing, problem-solving
- Size: Growing with right-to-repair movement

**Creative Professionals (10% of market)**
- Demographics: Designers, architects, artists, engineers
- Needs: Professional quality, reliable deadlines
- Challenges: Quality consistency, technical expertise
- Value: Professional service, B2B reliability, NDA protection
- Size: Lower volume, higher value

## 5. REVENUE STREAMS

**For what value are customers willing to pay? How would they prefer to pay? How much does each revenue stream contribute?**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown

**1. Ready-Made 3D Models (20% of revenue)**

**Pricing Model:** Fixed price per product
- Small items: £6-12
- Medium items: £12-25
- Large items: £25-50
- XL items: £50-100+

**Revenue Drivers:**
- Product sales volume
- Average order value
- Seasonal peaks (gifts)
- Repeat purchases

**Margin:** 60-70% (design costs amortized over multiple sales)

---

**2. Bespoke Printing Service (35% of revenue)**

**Pricing Model:** Cost-based (material + time + finishing)
- Material volume × rate
- Print time × hourly rate
- Post-processing fees
- Delivery

**Revenue Drivers:**
- Order volume
- Average print size
- Material mix (resin higher margin)
- Rush service premiums

**Margin:** 40-50% (material costs, machine time, labor)

---

**3. File Fixing & Repair (15% of revenue)**

**Pricing Model:** Tiered pricing + hourly (complex)
- Simple fixes: £12-20 (flat fee)
- Moderate repairs: £20-40 (flat fee)
- Complex repairs: £40-80 (flat fee)
- Extreme cases: £25/hour

**Revenue Drivers:**
- Repair volume
- Complexity mix
- Bundle conversions (repair + print)

**Margin:** 70-80% (mostly labor, minimal material cost)

---

**4. Full 3D Design Service (30% of revenue)**

**Pricing Model:** Package pricing + hourly
- Basic: £50-150
- Standard: £150-350
- Professional: £350-800
- Enterprise: £800-2000+
- Hourly: £35-60/hour

**Revenue Drivers:**
- Project volume
- Average project size
- Design complexity
- Print bundling (15% print discount if we designed it)

**Margin:** 50-60% (designer salary/freelance costs)

---

### Break-Even Analysis

**Monthly Fixed Costs:** ~£12,000 (mid-range estimate)
**Average Margin:** 50%

**Break-Even Revenue:** £24,000/month

**Required Sales Mix (Example):**
- 50 Ready-Made orders @ £20 avg = £1,000

## Business Model Risks & Mitigation

### Key Risks

**Technology Risk:**
- 3D printing tech improving rapidly
- Home printers becoming more capable
- **Mitigation:** Focus on service differentiation (repair, design), maintain quality gap

**Competition Risk:**
- Larger players entering market
- Price competition from overseas
- **Mitigation:** Build strong brand, customer relationships, UK-based advantage (speed, support)

**Demand Risk:**
- Market adoption slower than expected
- Seasonal fluctuations
- **Mitigation:** Diversified services, B2B focus for stability, international expansion potential

**Operational Risk:**
- Equipment failure
- Quality issues
- Staff turnover
- **Mitigation:** Backup equipment, rigorous QC, strong training, good culture

**Financial Risk:**
- Cash flow challenges
- High upfront investment
- **Mitigation:** Phased growth, deposits for large projects, financial controls

## Strategic Questions to Answer

**Critical Decisions:**

1. **Build vs. Partner:** Do we buy all equipment or partner for specialty printing?
   - **Recommendation:** Start with FDM + SLA in-house, partner for SLS

2. **Generalist vs. Specialist:** Do we serve everyone or focus on a niche?
   - **Recommendation:** Start broad, identify and focus on highest-value segments

3. **Volume vs. Margin:** Do we prioritize order volume or profit per order?
   - **Recommendation:** Balance both; volume on Ready-Made, margin on Design/Repair

4. **Geographic:** UK-only or international?
   - **Recommendation:** UK-only initially, international shipping Phase 2-3

5. **B2B vs. B2C:** Which should be primary focus?
   - **Recommendation:** 60% B2C, 40% B2B; B2B offers stability, B2C offers volume

## Conclusion

Quirkable's business model is built on providing comprehensive 3D printing solutions that competitors don't offer as an integrated package. By combining Ready-Made products, Bespoke printing, File Repair expertise, and Full Design services, Quirkable creates multiple revenue streams and customer touchpoints.

**The model's strength lies in:**
- Solving real customer pain points (broken files, no design skills)
- Creating customer loyalty through quality and service
- Scalable operations with both digital and physical products
- Defensible expertise (file repair, design) that creates barriers to entry

**Success depends on:**
- Executing with quality and consistency
- Building strong brand and community
- Managing costs while scaling
- Maintaining customer-obsessed culture
