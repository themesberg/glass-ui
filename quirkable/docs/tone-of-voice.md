# Tone of Voice

**How Quirkable Communicates**

## Brand Voice Principles

Quirkable speaks like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be brilliant at 3D printing. We're confident without being arrogant, technical without being intimidating, and creative without being chaotic.

---

## The Five Pillars of Quirkable's Voice

### 1. Smart
**What it means:**
We know our craft inside and out. We're technically proficient, detail-oriented, and always learning.

**What it sounds like:**
- Clear explanations of technical processes
- Confident recommendations based on experience
- Educational content that genuinely helps
- Respectful of the customer's intelligence

**Example:**
> "Your file has a few non-manifold edges at the base. Don't worry â€“ it's a common export issue from certain CAD programs, and we can clean it up in about 10 minutes."

**Not like this:**
> "Your file is broken and won't work." (Too blunt, not helpful)
> "The algorithmic topology presents suboptimal manifold geometry requiring mesh reconstruction." (Too technical, alienating)

---

### 2. Playful
**What it means:**
We love what we do. There's joy in creativity, personality in our work, and a sense of fun in the possibilities of 3D printing.

**What it sounds like:**
- Lighthearted language where appropriate
- Enthusiasm for customer projects
- Occasional wit and wordplay
- Celebrating the quirky and creative

**Example:**
> "Got a wobbly STL file? We've seen worse. We'll fix it up and have it printing beautifully."

**Not like this:**
> "We can repair malformed geometry." (Too dry)
> "OMG your file is SO broken lol but we got u fam! ðŸ˜‚" (Too casual, unprofessional)

---

### 3. Technically Sharp
**What it means:**
Precision matters. We understand the science, the materials, the processes. We get the details right.

**What it sounds like:**
- Accurate technical information
- Specific recommendations with reasoning
- Attention to detail in descriptions
- Competent, reliable, trustworthy

**Example:**
> "For this design, we'd recommend tough resin over standard. The thin hinge sections need the extra flexibility to avoid snapping during use."

**Not like this:**
> "This material is probably better, I think." (Vague, uncertain)
> "Per specification ASTM-D638, the tensile strength modulus..." (Over-technical for most contexts)

---

### 4. Customer First
**What it means:**
Everything we do is in service of helping the customer succeed. We listen, we solve problems, we care about their projects.

**What it sounds like:**
- Empathetic to customer challenges
- Solution-oriented communication
- Transparent about processes and pricing
- Proactive in addressing concerns

**Example:**
> "Your deadline is tight, but we can make it work. We'll prioritize your print and send you progress photos by Thursday so you can relax knowing it's on track."

**Not like this:**
> "Rush orders cost extra." (Transactional, not empathetic)
> "Whatever you want, we'll do it!" (Lacks boundaries, unsustainable)

---

### 5. Dependable
**What it means:**
We do what we say we'll do. We're consistent, reliable, and honest. Customers can trust us with their projects and their deadlines.

**What it sounds like:**
- Clear commitments and timelines
- Honest about limitations
- Consistent follow-through
- Taking responsibility

**Example:**
> "We'll have your quote to you by end of day tomorrow. If anything changes, we'll let you know immediately."

**Not like this:**
> "We'll try our best!" (Lacks commitment)
> "Guaranteed to be perfect or your money back, 100%, no questions asked, we're the best!" (Overpromising)

**Tone:** Calm, solution-oriented, transparent

**Style:**

- Explain why it happened (briefly)
- Provide the solution or next steps

## Word Choices

### Words We Love
- Create, build, design, sculpt, refine
- Precision, quality, care, detail
- Custom, bespoke, unique, original
- Fix, repair, optimize, improve
- Brilliant, beautiful, stunning
- Simple, easy, seamless, smooth

### Words We Avoid
- Cheap (use "affordable" or "budget-friendly")
- Perfect (use "high-quality" or "excellent")
- Revolutionary, game-changing (too hyperbolic)
- Industry-leading, world-class (clichÃ©)
- Never, always (too absolute)

## Grammar & Style Guidelines

### General Rules
- **Contractions:** Yes, use them. They sound natural and friendly.
  - "We'll fix that file" not "We will fix that file"
- **Oxford comma:** Yes, always.
  - "Material, finish, and delivery" not "Material, finish and delivery"
- **Exclamation points:** Sparingly. One per message maximum.
- **Emojis:** Occasional use in social media, never in formal communication
- **Questions:** Great for engagement, but don't overuse
  - "Need a custom design? Let's chat." âœ“

### Formatting
- **Bold** for emphasis on key points
- *Italics* for subtle emphasis or product names
- Bullet points for clarity
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Headers to break up long content

## Voice Don'ts

### Don't be condescending
**Bad:** "Well, if you had read the instructions, you would have known..."
**Good:** "The instructions can be a bit tricky â€“ here's a clearer explanation."

### Don't be overly technical without context
**Bad:** "Non-manifold edges and inverted normals detected in mesh topology."
**Good:** "Your file has a few technical issues (inside-out surfaces and gaps in the mesh), but we can fix them easily."

### Don't overpromise
**Bad:** "We'll have this done by tomorrow, guaranteed!"
**Good:** "We'll aim for tomorrow, but to be safe, expect it within 2 business days."

### Don't be robotic
**Bad:** "Your inquiry has been received and will be processed within 24 hours."
**Good:** "Thanks for reaching out! We'll get back to you within 24 hours."
